Project Overview

The main goal of this project was to explore buying patterns and customer behavior in an e-commerce environment. Instead of building a predictive model, this project focuses on business insights and actionable analytics by exploring trends across product categories, cities, age groups, payment methods, and ratings.

Business Questions Answered

  • Which product categories generate the most revenue?
  • Which cities contribute the most to sales?
  • Which age groups shop the most?
  • What are the most preferred payment methods?
  • How are customers rating their purchases?
  • Which categories have the highest vs. lowest satisfaction?

Key Findings

  • Electronics generated the most revenue but received the lowest average rating (~2.4 stars), indicating a quality or expectation gap.
  • Toys had the highest satisfaction (~3.8 stars) despite having lower sales, likely due to simpler functionality and lower customer expectations.
  • Netbanking was the most commonly used payment method, followed closely by Cash on Delivery, UPI, and lastly, Credit Card ‚Äî showing strong digital payment adoption. However, when making bigger purchases, customers still tend to use Credit cards, showing over $200k in revenue, despite the rise of online transactions.
  • Customers aged 46-60 made the most purchases, signaling a strong GenX buyer base.
  • City-level analysis showed clear differences in revenue contributions across locations, with Kolkata making the most in revenue of over $190k.
  • Ratings were centered around 3 stars, suggesting many ‚Äúneutral‚Äù or moderately satisfied customers.
