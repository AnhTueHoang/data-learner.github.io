In this project, I analyzed monthly retail sales data to identify long-term trends and seasonal patterns. I used time-series decomposition and moving averages to understand the structure of the data and prepare for forecasting future sales.

Project Overview

The goal of this analysis is to understand how sales change over time and whether the data contains repeatable patterns that support forecasting.

  • Is sales generally increasing or decreasing over time?
  • Is there repeating seasonality (peaks and dips each year)?
  • Are there unusual spikes or drops that stand out?

Process & Approach

  • Loaded monthly sales data and converted the Month column to a datetime index.
  • Plotted the time series to visually inspect trend and volatility.
  • Used seasonal_decompose to separate the series into trend, seasonality, and residual noise.
  • Computed a 12-month moving average to smooth short-term fluctuations and highlight long-term direction.

Key Insights from Visual Analysis

1) Overall Trend

Sales show a clear upward trend over time. This is visible in both the original time-series plot and the 12-month moving average, which steadily increases through the years.

2) Seasonality

The data shows strong yearly seasonality. Peaks and dips repeat at roughly the same time each year, and the seasonal component from decomposition confirms a consistent pattern.

3) Drops & Spikes

There are short-term spikes and dips, including a noticeable drop around 1962â€“1963. This period appears as a deviation from the typical pattern before sales recover later. It may reflect external market uncertainty, but without additional economic indicators, it should be treated as correlation rather than causation.
